Notice of Privacy Practices

This notice describes how medical information about you may be used and disclosed and how you can get access to this information. Please review it carefully.

GlucoBridge Privacy Notice

Effective Date: July 9, 2026 | HIPAA Compliant Standard

1. Introduction & Protected Health Information (PHI)

GlucoBridge is a health informatics web application designed to facilitate patient glycemic health tracking, remote monitoring, and clinical decision support. Because we collect, store, and process health telemetry, we are committed to protecting your Protected Health Information (PHI) in strict accordance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA).

2. Categories of Health Data We Collect

To provide accurate tracking and analysis, the application processes the following data classes:

Account Profiles

Usernames, authenticated email addresses, and core administrative credentials.

Clinical Encounters

Patient demographic fields, clinical encounter history notes, and provider metadata.

Health Telemetry

Vital signs and blood glucose readings recorded strictly via GlucoseLog data models.

3. Permitted Uses and Disclosures of Your PHI

Under HIPAA rules, your PHI is accessed and processed strictly for the following operational workflows:

  • Treatment & Monitoring: Generating individual glucose statistics, summary metrics, and trend lines for yourself and your linked care teams.
  • Clinical Safety Operations: Running server-side anomaly detection engines to flag glycemic spikes, dangerous trends, or critical anomalies, surfacing proactive alerts directly to clinical dashboards.
  • Health Informatics & Forecasting: Utilizing time-series forecasting models on your telemetry log history to generate short-term predictions of future blood glucose levels to assist in preventative management.

4. Legal Foundations & Access Governance

Access to PHI within GlucoBridge is bound to the strict principle of **Minimum Necessary Access**. Data visibility is partitioned through role-based access tokens; only explicitly authenticated clinicians or users with verifiable permissions can query patient files.

When data is utilized for clinical research or performance evaluation, it is completely separated from direct identifiers using the **HIPAA Safe Harbor De-identification Method** or restricted under an explicit **Data Use Agreement (DUA)** for Limited Data Sets.

5. Technical and Organizational Security

We implement rigorous administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of all electronic PHI (ePHI):

  • Encryption: Data encryption protocols are active for telemetry data both in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256).
  • Audit Logging: Every record access, export event, login, and profile modification is tracked via immutable backend audit trails.
  • Session Integrity: Automatic timeouts, strong password policies, and strict token-based authentication protect operational endpoints.

6. Right to Revoke Authorization

If you grant specific consent or authorization to share telemetry metrics for peripheral studies, **you retain the absolute right to revoke that authorization at any time**. Revoking your authorization stops future data processing or sharing immediately, though it cannot retroactively pull back data used while the consent was active. You can execute a revocation directly inside your Account Dashboard or by contacting the system administrator.

7. Your Individual Legal Rights

You hold the following federal rights regarding your protected health documentation within this application:

Right to Access & Inspect

You have the right to request and download a complete electronic export of your historical GlucoseLog histories and profile data.

Right to Restriction Request

You can request that we restrict how we use or share certain segments of your clinical data for treatment or operations (though we are not legally required to agree if it compromises vital tracking safety).

Right to an Accounting of Disclosures

You may request a formal log of instances where your health data was shared outside standard application treatment operations.

8. Contacts & Breach Notification Procedures

In the highly improbable event of a data breach involving unencrypted health data, GlucoBridge adheres to the federal **HIPAA Breach Notification Rule**. Affected individuals and regional care system administrators will be notified formally within mandated legal timeframes.

For privacy inquiries, rights assertions, or formal complaints, please reach out to the project administration desk via the secure support channels established in your application portal.

Disclaimer: This Notice of Privacy Practices is maintained for internal application system compliance context. It should be routinely validated by an informatics legal expert to mirror evolving institutional policies and specific deployment infrastructure requirements.
